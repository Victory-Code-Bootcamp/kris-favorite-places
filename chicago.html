Welcome to Chicago!

Chicago's Architecture and Skyline

Chicago is known for its stunning architecture, with iconic buildings like the Willis Tower (formerly known as the Sears Tower), the John Hancock Center, and the Wrigley Building gracing the city's skyline. Visitors can take a guided tour of the city's architecture, explore the Chicago Riverwalk, or take a ride on the famous Chicago Architecture Foundation River Cruise.

Chicago's Food Scene

Chicago is a food lover's paradise, with a rich culinary heritage that includes deep-dish pizza, hot dogs, Italian beef sandwiches, and more. The city is also home to several Michelin-starred restaurants, including Alinea, Smyth, and Oriole. Visitors can explore the city's many neighborhoods and sample its diverse cuisine, from Greek food in Greektown to Mexican cuisine in Pilsen.

Chicago's Cultural Attractions

Chicago is home to several world-class museums and cultural institutions, including the Art Institute of Chicago, the Museum of Science and Industry, and the Field Museum. The city is also a hub for the performing arts, with several renowned theaters like the Chicago Theatre, the Goodman Theatre, and the Steppenwolf Theatre Company. Visitors can also catch a concert at one of the city's many music venues.

Chicago's Sports Scene

Chicago is a sports lover's paradise, with several professional sports teams calling the city home, including the Chicago Cubs and White Sox (baseball), the Chicago Bulls (basketball), and the Chicago Bears (football). Visitors can catch a game at one of the city's iconic stadiums, like Wrigley Field or Soldier Field, or explore the Chicago Sports Museum to learn more about the city's sports history.
